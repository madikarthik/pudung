Authentic Rural Experiences

Connect with Nature and Tradition

At Pudung Farms, we offer immersive experiences that go beyond typical tourist activities. Each experience is thoughtfully designed to connect you with the land, local traditions, and the quiet wisdom of rural life. Our activities change with the seasons, ensuring authentic engagement with farm life as it naturally unfolds.

Organic Farming

Get your hands dirty and experience the joy of working with soil. Learn organic farming techniques as you help plant, tend, or harvest seasonal crops under the guidance of our experienced farmers.

Duration: 2-3 hours
Group Size: Max 10 people
Difficulty: Moderate

Seed Saving Workshop

Learn the ancient art of saving seeds from our heirloom varieties. Understand the importance of seed sovereignty and biodiversity as you learn to collect, clean, and store seeds for future plantings.

Duration: 1.5 hours
Group Size: Max 8 people
Difficulty: Easy

Traditional Ploughing

Experience traditional farming methods as you learn about and participate in ploughing with bullocks. Discover why these time-honored techniques are still relevant in today's farming context.

Duration: 2 hours
Group Size: Max 6 people
Difficulty: Moderate

Relli River Trek

Embark on a guided trek from our farm to the beautiful Relli River. Walk through village paths, forested trails, and enjoy the serene environment while learning about local flora and fauna.

Duration: 4-5 hours
Group Size: Max 8 people
Difficulty: Moderate

Bird Watching

Wake up early and join our naturalist for a guided bird watching session. Kalimpong's diverse ecosystem is home to numerous bird species, from colorful sunbirds to majestic eagles.

Duration: 2-3 hours
Group Size: Max 6 people
Difficulty: Easy

Forest Bathing

Practice the Japanese art of shinrin-yoku or "forest bathing" in our nearby forest. This guided mindfulness practice helps you connect deeply with nature through all your senses.

Duration: 2 hours
Group Size: Max 10 people
Difficulty: Easy

Animal Care Session

Join our farm team as they care for our animals. Help feed goats, collect eggs from chickens, or assist with grooming. Learn about compassionate animal husbandry practices.

Duration: 1-2 hours
Group Size: Max 8 people
Difficulty: Easy

Cow Milking

Try your hand at the traditional art of hand milking. Learn about dairy processing and the importance of ethical dairy farming from our experienced handlers.

Duration: 1 hour
Group Size: Max 6 people
Difficulty: Easy

Beekeeping Introduction

Discover the fascinating world of bees with our beekeeping introduction. Learn about bee behavior, hive structure, and the crucial role bees play in our ecosystem and food production.

Duration: 1.5 hours
Group Size: Max 6 people
Difficulty: Easy

Traditional Crafts Workshop

Learn traditional crafts like bamboo weaving, pottery, or local textile techniques from skilled village artisans. Create your own souvenir to take home as a memory of your time with us.

Duration: 2-3 hours
Group Size: Max 8 people
Difficulty: Easy

Village Walk

Explore the nearby village with a local guide who will introduce you to community members, traditional homes, and the rhythm of daily life in rural Kalimpong.

Duration: 2 hours
Group Size: Max 10 people
Difficulty: Easy

Traditional Cooking Workshop

Join our farm chef to learn local recipes using fresh, seasonal ingredients from our farm. From momos to traditional curries, discover the flavors and techniques of regional cuisine.

Duration: 3 hours
Group Size: Max 8 people
Difficulty: Easy

What to Bring

Be Prepared for Your Farm Experience

Clothing

  • Comfortable, weather-appropriate clothing
  • Sturdy walking shoes or boots
  • Hat for sun protection
  • Light jacket or sweater (evenings can be cool)
  • Rain gear (during monsoon season)

Personal Items

  • Sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Personal medications
  • Water bottle (we provide refill stations)
  • Camera
  • Notebook and pen

Mindset

  • Curiosity and openness to learn
  • Respect for local customs and traditions
  • Patience (farm life moves at nature's pace)
  • Willingness to disconnect (limited connectivity)
  • Readiness to participate actively
