The Art of Seasonal Farming: A Year at Pudung

Experience the rhythm of farm life through the changing seasons. From spring plantings to winter preparations, discover how we work in harmony with nature's cycles.
Rajesh Kumar Farm Manager

The Rhythm of Seasons

At Pudung Farms, our agricultural calendar is deeply intertwined with the natural cycles of the seasons. Each period brings its own unique activities, challenges, and rewards, creating a harmonious dance between farmer and nature.

Spring: The Season of New Beginnings

As winter retreats and the soil warms, our farm comes alive with activity. This is the time for:

  • Preparing seedbeds and nurturing young plants in our greenhouse
  • Planning crop rotations to maintain soil health
  • Setting up irrigation systems for the growing season

Summer: Peak Growing Season

Summer brings long days and rapid growth, requiring constant attention to:

  • Daily monitoring and harvesting of vegetables
  • Natural pest management through companion planting
  • Maintaining proper irrigation during dry spells
"Farming is not just about growing crops; it's about nurturing a relationship with the land and understanding its rhythms."

Autumn: Harvest and Preparation

The autumn months are crucial for:

  • Harvesting and storing root vegetables
  • Collecting seeds for next year's planting
  • Preparing the soil for winter with cover crops

Winter: Rest and Planning

While the land rests, we focus on:

  • Maintaining greenhouse crops
  • Planning next year's crop rotation
  • Repairing and maintaining farm equipment

Sustainable Practices Throughout the Year

Our commitment to sustainable farming means we:

  • Use natural fertilizers and composting
  • Practice water conservation
  • Maintain biodiversity through mixed cropping

Looking Forward

As we continue our journey in sustainable farming, we remain committed to sharing our knowledge and experiences with visitors and the farming community. Through our farm stays and workshops, we invite you to experience this seasonal rhythm firsthand.
